Tourism in Mauritius
“The world is a book, and those who don't travel only read one page.” - St Augustine

Famous for its breathtaking beaches and natural landscape, Mauritius, a tiny island in the Indian Ocean, is one of the most visited destination in Africa. The tourism industry is a pillar to the Mauritian economy as it brings in foreign currencies needed for imports and provides direct and indirect jobs to thousands of people. Where do tourists come from?

Over 1 million tourists from more than 100 countries visited Mauritius in 2019.
There were more tourists from France than any other countries, with more than 30% of tourists residing in the Hexagon.

The total number of tourists coming to Mauritius has been steadily increasing since the year 2013.

Peak Season

December is the most popular month amoung tourists. That is no surprise considering summer runs from November to April and December is one of the hottest months in Mauritius. Moreover, a lot of people go on vacation to celebrate Christmas and the New Year.
On the other hand, July usually welcomes the least number of tourists. Again, this is no surprise since this is one of the coldest months.

Gender and Age

The ratio of male to female tourists is almost 100%.
For male tourists, about 11% were aged between 30 to 34 whereas for female tourists, about 12% were aged between 25 to 29

Expenditure Per Night

More money is spent on Accomodation than anything else. This represents over 60% of total expenditure (per night).
Tourists from China, USA and UAE spent more money than any other tourists (per night).

Impact of Covid-19 on Tourism

Like most countries around the world, Covid-19 greatly impacted the tourism sector in Mauritius. With borders closing here and abroad, the tourism sector took a big hit.
Only 20% of tourists visited Mauritius in 2020 compared to 2019.

However, with restrictions being eased around the world and the rollout of vaccines, the future of the tourism industry in Mauritius is looking bright.
